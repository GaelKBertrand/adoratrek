Spiti Valley Road Trip – 9 Days of Himalayan Magic

A thrilling journey through high Himalayan deserts and dramatic landscapes. Explore ancient monasteries, remote villages, and crystal-clear skies.
Return to list of roadtrips
01

Day 1: Arrive in Shimla

Altitude: 2,200 m

Arrive in the charming hill town of Shimla. Take time to relax, acclimatize, and explore Mall Road, The Ridge, or Jakhoo Temple. Overnight stay in Shimla.

Optional: Local sightseeing or rest day before the journey into the high Himalayas.

02

Day 2: Shimla to Chitkul

Distance: ~250 km | Drive Time: 8–9 hrs | Altitude: Chitkul – 3,450 m

Begin your journey into Kinnaur Valley. Drive alongside the Sutlej and Baspa Rivers into the remote Baspa Valley. Reach Chitkul, the last inhabited village near the Indo-Tibet border, known for its wooden houses and raw mountain beauty.

Route: Shimla – Kufri – Rampur – Sangla – Chitkul

Overnight stay: Chitkul or Sangla

03

Day 3: Chitkul to Kalpa

Distance: ~65 km | Drive Time: 3–4 hrs | Altitude: Kalpa – 2,960 m

A scenic short drive takes you to Kalpa, a tranquil village with breathtaking views of the Kinnaur Kailash range. Visit the Roghi suicide point, ancient temples, and enjoy the peaceful apple orchards around.

Route: Chitkul – Sangla – Reckong Peo – Kalpa

Overnight stay: Kalpa

04

Day 4: Kalpa to Tabo via Giu Village (Mummy Temple)

Distance: ~190 km | Drive Time: 7–8 hrs | Altitude: Tabo – 3,280 m

Today you leave Kinnaur and officially enter Spiti Valley. En route, stop at Giu Village, home to the naturally preserved 500-year-old mummy of a Buddhist monk. Continue to Tabo, home to the 1,000-year-old Tabo Monastery, often called the ‘Ajanta of the Himalayas’.

Route: Kalpa – Pooh – Nako – Giu – Tabo

Overnight stay: Tabo

05

Day 5: Tabo to Kaza via Dhankar Monastery

Distance: ~50 km | Drive Time: 3–4 hrs | Altitude: Kaza – 3,800 m

A relaxed day with short drives and stunning monasteries. Visit Dhankar Monastery, dramatically perched on a cliff above the confluence of the Spiti and Pin rivers. Later reach Kaza, the administrative capital of Spiti Valley. Explore the local market and acclimatize.

Route: Tabo – Dhankar – Kaza

Overnight stay: Kaza

06

Day 6: Kaza – Komic – Hikkim – Langza (High Altitude Villages)

Distance: ~50 km (circuit) | Altitude: Komic – 4,587 m | Hikkim – 4,440 m | Langza – 4,400 m

Today’s circuit covers Spiti’s iconic high-altitude villages: Komic – One of the highest villages in the world connected by road, Hikkim – Home to the world’s highest post office, Langza – Famous for fossils and the giant Buddha statue. Return to Kaza in the evening.

Overnight stay: Kaza

07

Day 7: Kaza to Chandertal via Key Monastery & Chicham Bridge

Distance: ~110 km | Drive Time: 6–7 hrs | Altitude: Chandertal – 4,270 m

Start with a visit to the iconic Key Monastery, Spiti’s largest and most photogenic monastery. Then cross the breathtaking Chicham Bridge, one of Asia’s highest suspension bridges. After crossing Kunzum La (4,551 m), reach the magical Chandertal Lake, also known as the Lake of the Moon. Short walk (1.5 km) to reach the lake.

Route: Kaza – Key – Chicham – Kunzum Pass – Chandertal

Overnight stay: Camps near Chandertal (luxury or alpine tents)

08

Day 8: Chandertal to Manali

Distance: ~120 km | Drive Time: 7–8 hrs | Altitude: Manali – 2,050 m

Descend from Spiti into Lahaul Valley through rugged terrain and waterfalls. Cross Gramphu and reach Manali via the Atal Tunnel. Evening at leisure in Manali.

Route: Chandertal – Batal – Chhatru – Atal Tunnel – Manali

Overnight stay: Manali

09

Day 9: Departure from Manali / Manali to Chandigarh (Optional)

Your Spiti Valley Road Trip concludes today. Depart from Manali. If opted, drive from Manali to Chandigarh (not included in the basic package cost and subject to additional charges/booking).

Journey concludes with unforgettable memories.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to do the Spiti Valley Road Trip?

The best time for the Spiti Valley Road Trip (via Shimla-Kaza-Manali) is from June to October. This is when both Kunzum Pass and Rohtang Pass are open, allowing for the complete circuit.

What is the difficulty level of this road trip?

The Spiti Valley Road Trip is rated as moderate (6/10) due to the high altitudes and challenging road conditions in certain sections. While it's not physically demanding like a trek, a good level of stamina and adaptability to mountain travel is required.

What kind of accommodation is provided during the road trip?

Accommodation will be in comfortable hotels, guesthouses, and homestays. At Chandertal, you will stay in alpine tents or luxury camps, depending on the package chosen and availability.

Do I need prior experience for this road trip?

No prior experience in high-altitude travel is strictly mandatory, but it is advised to consult your doctor if you have any pre-existing medical conditions. A positive and adventurous spirit is key!

What is the maximum altitude reached on this road trip?

The highest point reached on the Spiti Valley Road Trip is Kunzum Pass, at an altitude of approximately 4,551 meters (14,931 feet), followed closely by Komic village at 4,587 meters (15,049 feet).

What kind of food will be provided during the road trip?

Our package includes breakfast and dinner throughout the trip. Meals will be a mix of local Himachali and Indian cuisine, provided at your accommodations. Lunch is usually at local eateries along the way (self-paid).

What if I face altitude sickness?

Our tour leaders are trained to recognize and manage symptoms of acute mountain sickness (AMS). We carry basic first aid and oxygen cylinders for emergencies. The itinerary is designed for gradual acclimatization, but it's crucial to inform your guide immediately if you feel unwell.

What is the typical group size for the Spiti Valley Road Trip?

The typical group size for this road trip ranges from a minimum of 6 people to a maximum of 40 people per departure, ensuring a dynamic group experience.
