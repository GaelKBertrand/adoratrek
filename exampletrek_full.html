Dzo Jongo with Markha Valley Trek

Mountaineering
Ladakh
01

Arrival in Leh

Altitude: 3,500 m

Fly into Leh, the capital of Ladakh. The high altitude requires immediate acclimatization. We will transfer you to your hotel. Spend the day resting to allow your body to adjust. This is critical for a safe and enjoyable trek. In the evening, you can take a light stroll through Leh's main bazaar.

Highlights: Arriving in a high-altitude desert city, first views of the Stok range.

10

Summit Day! Ascent of Dzo Jongo (6,250 m)

Altitude: 6,250 m | Trek: 10-12 hours

The ultimate challenge. We start post-midnight, navigating by headlamp. The initial section is over moraine, followed by a glacier crossing where we will be roped-up for safety. The climb is physically demanding and mentally challenging. The final ascent to the summit ridge rewards you with breathtaking 360-degree views. After soaking in the achievement, we descend carefully back to base camp.

Highlights: Unparalleled views of Kang Yatse, Stok Kangri, and distant giants like K2 (on a clear day).

13

Departure from Leh

After breakfast, check out from the hotel. We will drop you at the airport for your flight back home, filled with memories of an incredible adventure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What level of fitness is required?

This is a difficult trek (rated 7/10). You need excellent cardiovascular endurance. We recommend being able to run 10km in 60 minutes and having prior experience of at least one multi-day Himalayan trek.

What about Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)?

AMS is a genuine risk. Our itinerary is designed for gradual acclimatization. Our trek leaders are trained to identify and manage AMS symptoms. We carry emergency oxygen and have evacuation protocols in place. It's crucial you stay hydrated and communicate any discomfort to your guide immediately.
