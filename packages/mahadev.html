Shrikhand Mahadev Trek

A challenging high-altitude pilgrimage trek
Himachal Pradesh
Return to list of treks
01

Arrival at Manali / Nirmand

Altitude: 2,050 m (Manali) / 1,190 m (Nirmand)

Arrive at Manali (or directly to Nirmand if arranged). Meet the trek leader, complete paperwork and gear checks. Travel to Jaon village by road. Brief orientation about high-altitude pilgrimage and safety protocols.

Highlights: Introduction to local culture, final gear check, preparatory acclimatization briefing.

02

Jaon to Singhaad

Altitude Gain: From ~1,900 m to ~2,600 m | Distance: ~8 km

Start trek from Jaon through forested trails to Singhaad base camp. Free meal (langar) available. Early rest to aid acclimatization.

Highlights: Lush deodar forests, first views of high ridges.

03

Singhaad to Thaachru

Altitude Gain: From ~2,600 m to ~3,200 m | Distance: ~12 km

A steep uphill stretch known locally as 'Dandi-Dhaar'. Trek through a continuous incline; stops at scenic spots. Camp at Thaachru.

Highlights: Dramatic elevation gain, panoramic views of valleys below.

04

Kali Ghati to Bheem Talai

Altitude Gain: To ~3,500 m | Distance: ~6 km

Trek through forests and meadows to Kali Ghati, believed to be the abode of Goddess Kali. If weather is clear, glimpses of the Shivalingam at distance appear.

Highlights: Sacred site, spiritual atmosphere, view of distant peak.

05

Bheem Talai to Parvati Bagh

Altitude Variable: Around ~3,300–3,400 m | Distance: ~5 km

Descend slightly to Bheem Talai. This camp offers rest before further ascent. Terrain becomes rocky; stay alert.

Highlights: Rest stop with terrain variety.

06

Parvati Bagh to Bheem Dwar

Altitude Gain: To ~4,000 m | Distance: ~6 km

Ascend to Parvati Bagh, a lush meadow said to be planted by Goddess Parvati. Camp and rest; enjoy alpine flowers like Brahma Kamal if in season.

Highlights: Meadow camping, rich flora, spiritual vibes.

07

Summit Day: Bheem Dwar to Shrikhand Mahadev Peak (Nain Sarovar & Shivalingam)

Altitude: ~5,660 m | Trek Duration: 8–10 hours round trip

Pre-dawn start. Trek to Nain Sarovar (holy lake) around ~5,300 m, then the final rocky ascent to the Shivalingam at ~5,660 m (18,570 ft). Spend time in pilgrimage rituals at the peak. Descend carefully back to Parvati Bagh or Bheem Talai based on timing and conditions.

Highlights: Holy lake dip, Shivalingam worship at summit, panoramic Himalayan views.

08

Descent to Jaon / Base

Distance: Long descent over varied terrain | Altitude: From high camps down to Jaon

Descend through camps (Parvati Bagh Bheem Talai Kali Ghati Thaachru Singhaad Jaon). Each stage involves long walking hours; maintain steady pace to avoid strain.

Highlights: Final views, farewell to mountains, sense of accomplishment.

09

Return & Departure

Transfer from Jaon back to Manali or onward destination. Debrief, share feedback, and depart with memories of a transformative pilgrimage.

Highlights: Celebration of completion, local refreshments, group photo.

Frequently Asked Questions

What level of fitness is required?

This is a strenuous high-altitude trek requiring very good cardiovascular endurance. Prior multi-day trekking experience above 3,500 m is strongly recommended.

What about Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)?

AMS is a real risk on this route. Our itinerary allows gradual acclimatization; guides monitor symptoms closely. Emergency oxygen and evacuation plan are in place. Stay hydrated and report any discomfort immediately.

What is included?

All camping equipment, tents, sleeping bags, cooked meals during trek, local permits, guide and porter support. Transportation from Manali/Nirmand to trailhead and back is included.

Trek Essentials

  • Warm layers (down jacket, thermal wear) suitable for sub-zero nights.
  • Sturdy trekking boots, gaiters, and trekking poles.
  • Daypack with rain cover, water bottles, and hydration system.
  • Personal medication, first-aid kit, sunscreen, sunglasses.
  • Headlamp with spare batteries, power bank, camera.
  • Permits and ID proofs (will be arranged but keep copies).
  • Minimum 5 people, maximum 20 people per group.
