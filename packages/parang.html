Detailed Itinerary

01

Manali to Kaza

Duration: Drive

Your journey begins with a scenic drive from Manali to Kaza, the sub-divisional headquarters of Spiti Valley. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and spend the day acclimatizing to the high altitude.

Highlights: Scenic drive through high mountain passes (Rohtang Pass/Atal Tunnel, Kunzum Pass), acclimatization in Kaza.

02

Kaza to Kibber (4,205m) to Chicham to Get to Langza

Duration: Acclimatization/Sightseeing

Spend the day exploring Kaza and its surroundings. Visit Key Monastery, Kibber village (one of the highest inhabited villages), Chicham Bridge (Asia's highest suspension bridge), and the fossil village of Langza. This day is crucial for further acclimatization.

Highlights: Key Monastery, Kibber, Chicham Bridge, Langza fossil village, crucial acclimatization.

03

Kaza to Pangmo (4,300m)

Duration: ~4-5 hrs trek

The first trekking day begins from Kaza. A gradual ascent leads you to the beautiful campsite of Pangmo, offering panoramic views of the Spiti Valley landscape.

Highlights: First day of trekking, scenic Spiti Valley views, camping at Pangmo.

04

Pangmo to Jagtha (4,450m)

Duration: ~5-6 hrs trek

Today's trek follows the course of the Parilungbi river. The trail is mostly flat with some gradual ascents, taking you deeper into the barren yet beautiful Trans-Himalayan landscape.

Highlights: Trekking along Parilungbi river, experiencing the desert landscape.

05

Jagtha to Dak Karzong (4,900m)

Duration: ~6-7 hrs trek

A long day with a gradual but consistent ascent. The landscape becomes even more stark and high-altitude desert-like. Dak Karzong campsite is situated in a remote, high-altitude setting, providing a sense of wilderness.

Highlights: Remote high-altitude camping, stark mountain views.

06

Dak Karzong to Parang La (5,580m) to Kharsa Gongma (5,150m)

Duration: ~9-10 hrs trek

This is the summit day and the most challenging part of the trek. A steep and strenuous ascent leads to the high Parang La Pass. From the pass, enjoy breathtaking views before a long and often steep descent, sometimes through snow, to Kharsa Gongma campsite.

Highlights: Summiting Parang La, panoramic views of Ladakh and Spiti ranges, challenging descent.

07

Kharsa Gongma to Tso Moriri (4,593m)

Duration: ~5-6 hrs trek

Today's trek leads you to the stunning turquoise waters of Tso Moriri, one of the largest high-altitude lakes in India. The trail follows the course of the Pare Chu river, eventually opening up to the majestic lake.

Highlights: Reaching the beautiful Tso Moriri Lake, scenic riverside trek.

08

Explore around Tso Moriri

Duration: Rest/Acclimatization day

A well-deserved rest day to explore the mesmerizing surroundings of Tso Moriri. You can walk along the lake shore, visit the nomadic settlements, and absorb the serene beauty of this remote region. This day also aids further acclimatization.

Highlights: Relaxing by Tso Moriri, exploring nomadic culture, birdwatching.

09

Tso Moriri to Korzok (4,530m)

Duration: ~3-4 hrs trek

A relatively short and pleasant trek along the shores of Tso Moriri leads you to Korzok village, home to the ancient Korzok Monastery and a significant nomadic settlement by the lake.

Highlights: Trek along Tso Moriri, visit Korzok village and monastery.

10

Korzok to Leh

Duration: Drive

After breakfast in Korzok, embark on a scenic drive to Leh, the capital of Ladakh. The journey will take you through stunning high-altitude desert landscapes, marking the conclusion of your epic Parang La Trek.

Highlights: Scenic drive through remote Ladakh, arrival in Leh.

Frequently Asked Questions

What level of fitness is required for Parang La Trek?

The Parang La Trek is rated Challenging. It involves crossing a very high-altitude pass, long trekking days in remote and barren landscapes, and significant altitude gains. Participants must have excellent physical fitness, prior high-altitude trekking experience, and be well-prepared for extreme conditions. Regular cardio and strength training are essential.

What is included in the trek package?

Inclusions: Camping gear, all meals during trek, experienced guide, permits, trek team support, transport from Manali to Kaza and Korzok to Leh.

What is NOT included?

Exclusions: Porterage, hotel stays in Manali, Kaza, Leh.

Trek Essentials

  • Sturdy trekking boots with ankle support and good grip (waterproof recommended).
  • Layered clothing system (base layers, warm fleece, waterproof/windproof outer shell, down jacket for extreme cold).
  • Large backpack (50-60L) and a smaller daypack (20-30L).
  • Headlamp/torch with spare batteries.
  • Sunscreen (high SPF), lip balm, glacier glasses/sunglasses (UV protected), and a wide-brimmed cap/hat.
  • Personal first-aid kit with basic medications for headache, fever, stomach issues, bandages, antiseptic, etc.
  • Water bottles/hydration bladder (at least 2L capacity) and water purification tablets/filter.
  • Rain poncho or waterproof jacket/pants.
  • Thermal wear (top and bottom).
  • Woolen socks (multiple pairs) and warm gloves/mittens.
  • Toiletries and personal hygiene items (eco-friendly soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, small towel).
  • Trekking poles (highly recommended for stability and reducing strain).
  • Identity proof and photocopies.
  • Power bank for charging electronics.
